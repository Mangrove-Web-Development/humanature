Design for Humanity

We are a design strategy firm that works with leaders and their organizations to put humans at the center.

What We're About

We’ve been called many things: Futurists, Policy Advocates, Scientist-Practitioners. But we think of our work more broadly. We imagine a world that supports the whole of humanity and we help shape the equitable processes that will take us there. 

Our practice designs spaces and models that foster a more engaged, inclusive, productive, and overall human-centered approach to your work. We offer leadership-based strategies and systems-level frameworks to create more equitable organizations that promote dynamic power-sharing, sustainable relationships with communities, and the overall resiliency of humanity. 

Our solutions draw from tried-and-true methods in the indigenous world, along with groundbreaking social-science research in fields like neurology, ontology, and epistemology. 

At Humanature, we believe that all of this knowledge is paramount to how we, as a world, think about human development, belongingness, and the Future of Work.

Humanature Methodology

At Humanature, our work is grounded in a methodology that we call “Design for Humanity”.

Coming from backgrounds as researchers and practitioners in public health, we draw from an ever-expanding scientific literature around the human brain, social mapping, eco-toxicology, and decolonization. We implement those lessons in the real world through the creation of policy, dialogue, and collaborative spaces that take into account both the mechanics of our humanity and historical barriers to access and equity.

 

By adopting a far-reaching and inclusive view of humanity, we support leaders and organizations in pursuit of an equitable future. We offer frameworks and tools to foster long-term alignment between missions and outcomes, so that organizations do more than just “signal” their values. Our clients actually get results. Our work is heavily informed by the science of learning, identity-based experiences, and the impacts of stress and trauma. 

  • Cognitive Science
  • Biology
  • Human Development
  • Adult Learning
